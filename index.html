Process

The process of specifying and finishing a landscape design project might take different routes depending on the scale, budget, requirements and purpose. In the next few lines all the different stages are explained in order to create a clearer image of what landscape design actually consists of. We are happy to collaborate and often do so with architects, interior designers and developers and also aim to work on various public or private projects.

1 | Brief

Telephone/email correspondence is followed up by an initial meeting which helps us visualise client’s general requirements and initiate the terms and conditions of the future contract.

2 | Survey

Once we have been officially appointed by the client and a written brief has been issued we would normally come on site to carry on analysis (soil, geo location, site aspect etc.) and get a better sense of space

3 | Initial Proposal

In this stage we are in the position to create our initial vision of the space. Our first proposal is issued as a pdf design package and includes initial sketch master plan, 3D sketch visual graphics and a supporting mood board to help us convey our ideas as clearly as possible.

4 | Final proposal

After the design has been confirmed by both sides we will issue our final design proposal and will carry on with the process of completion which involves producing a detailed technical package of all the hard and soft landscaping of the project.

5 | Construction and Project Overseeing

We have established good connections with one of the best contractors, sculptors, artisans and suppliers to ensure the completed project is up to our high standards and the client is as satisfied as possible at the very end of our relationship. The overseeing of the project involves us being on site during construction and minimizing client’s involvement in the process. That’s our way of providing our clients with hassle free user experience and greater satisfaction.

Work

1 | West London

City oasis garden escape opens up this Tudor estate in West London. The design is unusually modern with and English cottage style twist. Chanticleer pears frame the garden and serve as columns of the two dimensional existing site. Cloud pruned Buxus sempervirens hedge features emphasise the destination points of the garden and create textural contrast together with the ornamental grasses and perennials such as Polystichum setiferum, Stipa tenuissima, Allium giganteum, Carex ‘Everest’, Papaver orrientalis ‘Patty’s Plum’. There are hidden secrets spots within the garden bringing Asian and Pasific feel with tree ferns Dicksonia antarctica and Japanese maple Acer palmatum.

4 | Courtyard Garden

Small courtyard garden accommodates an expansive exotic hardwood deck area suitable for sun bathing in hot summer days. A stepping stone and gravel pathway planted with ferns and grasses creates this contemporary feel against the traditional presence of the Victorian mansion house. Plating palette consists of unusual leafy and elegant combinations of Pittosporum ‘Silver Queen’, Cornus alba ‘Elegantissima’ and Cotinus ‘Grace’. Ornamental grasses soften the transition between the planting bed and the lawn and a Buxus sempervirens hedge frames the composition in typical Victorian way.

2 | Large Town Garden

Large town garden is hidden behind a Cottage style London property. The garden features an entertaining curved seating area to accommodate evening parties. Surrounded by lush layers of planting and contemporary fragrant Canadian cedarwood trelliswork the entertaining area is cozy and inviting. The rest of the garden consist of playground lawn and a dramatic car park front entrance laid in cutting edge silver grey granite planks and dark fine aggregate in a complete contrast to the traditional front façade of the house.

3 | Uncommon River

After winning 1st place of the competition ‘Uncommon River’ our project was built for the opening of the annual One Architecture Week in Plovdiv, Bulgaria. We wanted to create a shady and cozy atmosphere where locals could sit and enjoy the view across the Maritza river, which was the pure intention of the competition – to drive attention to the neglected river banks and to regenerate its Northern embankment. Without obstructing the natural habitat we encourage people to come and observe the surrounds of this quiet river embankment surrounded by peaceful timber slat work casting poetic, latticed shadow. Available budget for the construction was very limited therefore we had to be as creative as possible to be able to convey our ideas through to the end user. After several returns to the space we were quite pleased to have found out that the pergola was actively used and enjoyed by locals creating their new secret spot across the melancholic and forgotten area they reside in.

5 | Japanese Garden Retreat – Work in progress

This small garden features contemporary looking granite setts terrace and a comfy dining set suitable for outdoor parties all year round due to the nearby outdoor fireplace. The accent of the terrace are the splendid handmade round clay pots planted with fresh looking chunks of Buxus. At the very end of the terrace sits the Low Japanese tea room where you can only crawl in and have a seat. It is designed to be a getaway from the noisy and busy outer world surrounded by peaceful layers of Japanese gravel and stepping stones and two specimen multistem trees. The far right corner of the garden contains our client’s favorite sculpture seated within table of Buxus of different heights.
